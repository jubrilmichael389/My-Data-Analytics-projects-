Project Overview: 
This project analyzes product-level sales data to evaluate performance across categories, regions, and time. The aim is to uncover revenue drivers, underperforming items, and opportunities for business growth using data-driven insights.

Objectives:
Identify top and bottom performing products by revenue and quantity sold
Analyze trends in product sales over time
Compare performance across product categories and regions
Detect slow-moving products
Support business decision-making with insights

Dataset: 
Source: Sample / practice dataset
Description: Transactional sales data containing product name, category, order date, quantity, unit price, region, and revenue
Records: Multiple rows across different time periods.

Tools & Technologies: 
SQL Server (data cleaning & transformation)
Microsoft Excel (exploratory analysis)
Power BI (dashboard design & visualization)

Analysis Process: 
Inspected the dataset for missing values and duplicates.
Standardized product names, categories, and date formats.
Created calculated fields for revenue and sales volume.
Aggregated metrics by product, category, and region.
Applied ranking logic to identify top and bottom performers.
Built interactive Power BI dashboards with slicers and KPIs.

Key Insights: 
A small group of products contributed a significant share of total revenue.
Certain categories consistently outperformed others.
Sales showed seasonal patterns across months.
Some products had declining performance and require attention.

Recommendations:
Increase marketing investment for high-performing products.
Review pricing or promotions for underperforming items.
Optimize inventory levels by category and region.
Introduce bundles or cross-selling strategies.
